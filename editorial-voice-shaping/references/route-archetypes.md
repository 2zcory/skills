# Route Archetypes

Use the nearest archetype before rewriting. One route can blend two archetypes, but one should still lead.

## Orientation

- Primary job: help the visitor choose where to go next
- Typical surfaces: `Home`, top-level landing pages
- Bias toward:
  - fast orientation
  - clear route distinctions
  - low theory density

## Background

- Primary job: give personal or product context without competing with the work
- Typical surfaces: `About`
- Bias toward:
  - context after the fact
  - lower manifesto energy
  - personal texture without profile theater

## Editorial

- Primary job: prepare the reader for slower, more deliberate reading
- Typical surfaces: `Writing`
- Bias toward:
  - calmer pacing
  - clearer weight hierarchy
  - less explanation of editorial theory

## Open Thread

- Primary job: keep unfinished thought legible without pretending it is settled
- Typical surfaces: `Garden`
- Bias toward:
  - openness
  - adjacency
  - live movement without poetic overreach

## Proof

- Primary job: show work and outcomes with enough frame to read them correctly
- Typical surfaces: `Projects`
- Bias toward:
  - scanning
  - proof over theater
  - hierarchy without over-explaining the hierarchy

## Channel Boundary

- Primary job: invite the right contact while staying honest about access limits
- Typical surfaces: `Contact`
- Bias toward:
  - clarity
  - useful next steps
  - honest limits without procedural coldness
