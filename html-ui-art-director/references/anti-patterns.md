# Anti-Patterns

Run this critique before presenting the artifact.

## Card Soup

Symptom:

- many similar boxes with equal weight
- no dominant surface

Fix:

- merge weak surfaces
- remove unnecessary framing
- promote one region to hero and calm the rest

## Radius Drift

Symptom:

- radius appears everywhere without structural reason
- the whole UI feels soft by habit rather than intent

Fix:

- define a radius policy
- reduce radius on structural surfaces
- keep stronger rounding only where touch or friendliness matters

## Fake Premium

Symptom:

- blur, glow, and gradient effects try to replace hierarchy

Fix:

- strengthen composition first
- reduce decorative effects until structure still works in grayscale

## Flat Hierarchy

Symptom:

- every section feels equally important
- first view has no clear reading path

Fix:

- enlarge or intensify only the main decision area
- compress support content
- remove duplicate CTAs

## Asset Noise

Symptom:

- icons, images, and logos all compete at once

Fix:

- choose one primary signal source
- simplify the rest

## Template Sameness

Symptom:

- the page feels like a generic startup template
- the design could belong to any product

Fix:

- sharpen the visual language
- rewrite hero content to reflect the actual product
- change the layout archetype if needed

## Weak Mobile Collapse

Symptom:

- desktop composition disappears on small screens
- support content pushes the action too far down

Fix:

- re-rank sections for mobile
- shorten copy
- remove or defer nonessential surfaces

## Critique threshold

If the artifact triggers two major anti-patterns, revise before presenting.

If it triggers three, the layout or visual language choice is probably wrong.
