# Design Critique Rubric

Score the artifact after layout and styling decisions are in place.

Use a `1` to `5` score for each dimension:

- `1`: weak or clearly wrong
- `3`: acceptable but ordinary
- `5`: strong and intentional

## 1. Hierarchy

Check:

- is the first thing to notice obvious?
- does the eye know where to go next?
- are support surfaces quieter than the hero?

## 2. Layout Clarity

Check:

- does the layout match the screen job?
- is the first viewport structurally legible?
- would the structure still work with less styling?

## 3. Visual Language Coherence

Check:

- do type, radius, shadow, and color feel like one direction?
- is the page using one authored voice rather than mixed defaults?

## 4. Asset Strategy

Check:

- do icons, logos, imagery, or placeholders serve a clear role?
- is there one primary trust or atmosphere signal?

## 5. Anti-Generic Strength

Check:

- would this page still feel specific if the copy changed?
- has the design escaped marketplace-template sameness?
- has the layout avoided the most common default pattern for this screen category?

## 6. CTA And Decision Clarity

Check:

- is the next step obvious?
- are secondary actions clearly secondary?

## 7. Mobile Integrity

Check:

- does the hierarchy survive on narrow screens?
- does support content stay out of the way of the main action?
- if this is a mobile-first flow, does it feel intentionally composed rather than like desktop content squeezed into a phone frame?

## Scoring bands

- `29-35`: strong reference quality
- `22-28`: usable but needs another refinement pass
- `15-21`: structurally weak; revisit layout choice
- `<15`: restart from screen job and archetype selection

## Failure triggers

Even with a decent score, revise before presenting if any of these are true:

- no clear dominant surface
- three or more equal-weight sections above the fold
- imagery or icons feel decorative rather than structural
- the mobile stack destroys the first decision
